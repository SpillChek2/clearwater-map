ClearWater in Ecuador

ClearWater is bringing clean drinking water to indigenous communities in the Ecuadorian Amazon. By building rainwater catchment systems, ClearWater works with communities to provide viable clean water solutions.

Read about where we work ▼

The Nor-Oriente Amazon

The Amazon rainforest produces 20% of our water. In the Ecuadorian Amazon, the water sources are contaminated by decades of reckless oil operations and 30,000 peoples are suffering a health crisis due to the toxins in the water. ClearWater is partnering with five indigenous tribes to directly address this crisis.

Meet the Cofán ▼

Cofán Dureno

Centuries ago, the Cofán people moved down from the foothills of the Andes Mountains to inhabit a large territory in the northern Ecuadorian Amazon. The Cofán have been inspiring protagonists in the 19-year legal battle against the oil companies, yet they continue to suffer from the devastating contamination in their communities, most significantly the lack of clean water.

Gonzálo Criollo is the son of renowned Cofán shaman Rufino Criollo, and brother of Cofán musicians Silverio and Claus Criollo. Meet the Secoya ▼

Secoya

  • + XX Rainwater Catchment Systems
  • + XX people benefitted
  • + x community technicians trained

The Secoya suffer heavily from oil-related contamination. Rivers have been contaminated, making much of the water unsafe to drink or bathe in; traditional subsistence activities like hunting, fishing, and growing edible crops are at risk. Despite the challenges, the Secoya are highly organized and dedicated to building clean water solutions for their people.
